About This Workbook

This pamphlet offers a set of exercises, including prompts for a daily research and reflection journal, to guide you in the Field Workshop program. The prompts are fully optional, but we hope they will sharpen your inquiry and give us, as a group, a shared foundation to draw from as we discuss our process throughout the workshop period.

We have also included a number of prompts that we hope you will respond to and hand in to us at the end of the Field Workshop, including a reflection on your synthesis group projects. Over three weeks, your reflections will become a record of your development as an integrative researcher.

How to use this workbook

Write in it freely. Cross things out. Make it yours. Return to the daily reflection page (Exercise 3) each evening.

The prompts are organized around the Integration Cycle from Integrate This! — Immerse, Diverge, Connect, Synthesize, Test, Reflect.

Some exercises are for individual reflection; others (Exercise 5) are for your synthesis team to complete together.

Exercises 4 and 5 are meant to be handed in at the end of the workshop.

Exercise 1: Coming from Elsewhere

We begin at the beginning, by placing ourselves in relation to the Field Workshop site. Most people who join this workshop are not from the San Luis Valley or the Taos Plateau. We are all arriving with credentials, curiosity, and good intentions — and an outside perspective, which are gifts to bring into our interactions and experiences within the region.

While it is possible that some of the communities we will meet have experience with researchers who arrive, extract, and leave, they may also have experienced researchers who arrived in the valley and became genuine long-term partners. While your time in this region may not extend past the Field Workshop, Wright-Ingraham will continue working in the region for the coming years, and you can anticipate that your research here will spark future collaborations and conversations.

What does it mean to you to come from outside the communities you are about to enter as a researcher?

What assumptions are you carrying about these places and the people who live and work in them — and where did those assumptions come from?

What do you have to offer these communities, honestly, as someone who is new here? What are the limits of what you can offer in three weeks?

What would you want a researcher to do — and not do — if they were coming to your home community to study something you care about? What would help you to trust them?

Exercise 2: Draft a Collaborative Agreement

In the first days of the Workshop, your cohort will develop a Collaborative Agreement: a shared document describing how you will work together. Your agreement should address how decisions will be made when team members disagree, how credit and authorship will be shared, how you will handle it when someone feels unheard or sidelined, what each team member brings as their primary lens and strength, and what each team member most wants to learn from the others.

Exercise 3: Daily Reflection Prompts

We suggest you use the daily reflection prompts as a practice to record your observations and experiences during the Field Workshop. These prompts recall our Integration Cycle. Return to this exercise each evening.

Date: ___________________________    Location: _________________________________________

Immerse
What did you observe, encounter, or experience today? What is your first, unfiltered impression of where you were and what you witnessed? What did your body notice before your mind caught up — the light, the scale, the texture of the place, the quality of a conversation?
Diverge
What different lenses or framings could you apply to what you saw? Whose perspective were you missing today? What disciplinary or community lens, if you had access to it, would make what you observed look completely different?
Connect
What surprised you? Where did different threads link? Where did something you heard or saw today connect unexpectedly with something from a different part of your experience — another field site, another conversation, something you read, something you already knew?
Synthesize
What is your provisional understanding today? Complete this sentence without overthinking it: 'Today I learned that…' Then: 'The question I'm carrying into tomorrow is…'
